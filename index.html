Vensia

A GTP-4o Generated World

Vensia, a land of vast diversity, from towering, mist-wreathed peaks
to ancient forests, rolling grasslands, and sun-bleached deserts.
The seas are fickle, sometimes a giver of bounty,
sometimes a cruel reaper of lives.


Locations




The Costal Kingdom
- A small but influential maritime nation, known for its bustling harbors, rugged cliffs, old, storm-battered towns, trade, shipbuilding, and seafaring traditions.
- Its fleet and skilled sailors make it a force to be reckoned with in trade and naval skirmishes.
- The People are hardy, practical, and deeply superstitious with a reverence for the sea and its many unknowns.
- Ruled by a merchant-prince council, it has a long history of piracy, naval warfare, and exploration.
- Dunmodrin -
- Great stone walls that are carved into the mountain, halls echoing with songs. A bustling port city where traders, mercenaries, and trouble flow in equal measure, with Maze-like alleys and lively taverns that exude a sense of mystery and opportunity.
  - The Rusted Anchor - A tavern meant for sailors needing a place to sleep off a drunk night.
  - The Wharfrest - A middle-of-the-road two story inn with weathered but well-maintained wooden facade and a quiet atmosphere.
  - The Gilded Osprey - A pricier inn near the docks, catering to wealthier merchants and captains.
- The Jagged Straits -
- A treacherous stretch of water separating the mainland of Ordwynne from the Blacktooth Cliffs. With rocky outcroppings, unpredictable waters, and unnatural mists.

- Blacktooth Cliffs -

- South of Ordwynne lies the kingdom of Veylan, a land of rolling golden plains, sprawling river valleys, and dense, ancient forests.
- Veylan is known for its warm climate, fertile farmland, and great trade cities that flourish along the banks of the Aelthwyn River.
- Veylan was once part of an old empire, and remnants of that past can still be seen in its towering aqueducts, crumbling fortresses, and forgotten roads that stretch deep into the wilderness.
- Though its capital, Rellisport, is a hub of commerce and culture, much of the country is still untamed, with vast stretches of land ruled by merchant lords, feuding noble houses, and isolated communities that keep to their own ways.
- Its people have their own share of myths—tales of shadowy figures in the old ruins, whispering spirits that haunt the riverbanks, and the mysterious Wardens of the Deepwood, a reclusive order said to guard secrets from before the empire’s fall.





The Silvered Expanse
- Velhannar is a land of constant movement—caravans, migrating herds, and warrior bands all shaping its ever-changing political and cultural landscape.
- A landlocked country of rolling steppes, vast rivers, and windswept plains, known for its skilled riders, wandering clans, and ancient oral traditions.
- Once ruled by a powerful, nomadic horse-lord empire, now fragmented into warring factions and independent city-states.
- Velhannar’s people are fiercely independent, valuing loyalty, strength, and the open sky over rigid laws.
- The great rivers that carve through the land make trade possible, but also serve as the borders between rival clans.
- The capital city, Althrekh, is a sprawling, fortified stronghold on the banks of the mighty Sovarr River, where warriors, traders, and mystics gather to seek fortune and wisdom.





The Highland Dominion
- A rugged, mountainous country to the north, known for its strong warriors, rich mines, and ancient fortresses.
- Home to many dwarves, goliaths, and hardy human clans, who live in self-sufficient holds.
- Governed by a system of clan lords who follow a High King, though infighting is common.





The Fractured Empire
- A once-great empire, now split into numerous warring city-states and noble houses.
- Known for its artistry, dueling culture, and arcane scholars, but also its cutthroat politics.
- Each principality is ruled by a Prince or Magelord, constantly vying for power.





The Southern Expanse
- A vast, sun-scorched land of golden deserts, sprawling trade cities, and lost ruins.
- Home to nomadic tribes, ancient mystics, and powerful sultanates.
- Rich in spices, rare metals, and old magic, but plagued by sandstorms and warbands.





The Verdant Enclave
- A lush, deeply forested country, home to elves, druids, and reclusive mystics.
- Said to hold sacred groves, hidden cities, and ancient fey portals.
- Outsiders are rarely welcomed, as the land is protected by both magic and tradition.





The Frozen Wastes
- A frozen, barren land at the world’s northern edge, where survival is a battle against nature itself.
- Populated by fierce raiders, ice sorcerers, and creatures of the deep tundra.
- Legends speak of slumbering giants, lost kingdoms beneath the ice, and an eternal winter creeping southward.

Miscellaneous



The Calendar System
  Year: 865 A.R. (After Reckoning)
  Months: 10 months in a year
  Days: 36 days in each month, for a total of 360 days in a year
  Week Length: 6 days in a wee
Months of the Year (10 total, 36 days each)
  Thawtide – The month of thawing ice and the first signs of spring.
  Bloomreach – A time of full spring, when flowers and crops begin to flourish.
  Goldwane – The warming month, as the first harvests of early summer begin.
  Highsun – The peak of summer, marked by long days and strong heat.
  Scorchwane – Summer wanes, but the land still bakes under the sun.
  Harvestwane – The major harvest month, as the days begin to cool.
  Dusktide – The season of autumn’s full embrace, a time of preparation.
  Frostwane – The first chills of winter creep in, shortening the days.
  Deepfrost – The heart of winter, when the land lies dormant under snow.
  Embertide – A time of bitter cold, yet marked by fires, feasts, and renewal.
Days of the Week (6 total)
  Suntide – The first day, associated with renewal and beginnings.
  Stonehold – A day of labor and endurance.
  Wavecrest – Tied to travel, trade, and change.
  Moondark – A day of contemplation, magic, and the unknown.
  Ironfast – A time of fortitude and trials, often linked to battles or hardship.
  Hearthrest – A day of gathering, feasts, and reflection.
